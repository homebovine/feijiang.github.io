Research Overview

1, A semi-parametric transformation model for semi-competing risk survival data analysis.

A class of semi-parametric transformation model is proposed for analyzing semi-competing risk survival data. The semi-parametric efficient score and a kernel based non-parametric imputation method are combined for the parameter estimation.

2,Estimating the treatment difference with data from a comparative randomized clinical trial in presence of potential baseline-covariate imbalance.

A consistent and efficient augmented estimator for treatment effect is proposed and its large sample and finite sample properties are derived and evaluated.

3, A Bayesian decision-theoretic sequential-response adaptive randomization design.

The Bayesian decision-theoretic method and adaptive randomization are combined to design a phase II clinical trial. A constraint backward induction and forward simulation algorithm is developed for implementing the design.

4, A semi-parametric method for survival analysis, with application to a seamless phase II/III clinical trial design.

A second order semi-parametric estimation procedure and a non-parametric imputation method are combined to analyze survival data sets.

5, Fused kernel-spline smoothing for repeatedly measured outcomes in a generalized partially linear model with functional single index.

A single-index score model is proposed for a longitudinal data analysis. A fused kernel and B-spline technique is developed for the parameter estimation.

6, Randomization adapted to continuous and discrete covariates in clinical trials.

A kernel based continuous covariate adaptive allocation scheme is proposed. The asymptotic prop- erty of the procedure is derived by using the martingale techniques.

7, Survival function estimations under the stochastic ordering constraints.

A methodology for the survival function estimation under stochastic ordering constraints is pro- posed. The analytic form and the asymptotic properties of the estimator are derived.
